Parotta

Description

Parotta is a Subcontinental layered flatbread made from Maida or Atta, alternatively known as flaky ribbon pancake. It is very common in Kerala and Tamil Nadu

Porottas (Parathas) are often available as street food and in restaurants across Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. At some places it is also served at weddings, religious festivals and feasts. It is prepared by kneading maida/wheat flour, egg (in some recipes), oil or ghee and water. The dough is beaten into thin layers and later forming a round spiralled into a ball using these thin layers. The ball is rolled flat and pan fried.

Ingredients

  • Maida or atta
  • Egg(optional)
  • Salt
  • Preferable vegetable oil

Procedural Steps

  1. Take a bowl and add maida to the bowl.
  2. Add required amount of salt to it.
  3. Now,Add water and mix it untill it becomes a soft dough
  4. Wait for sometime and make a small balls from the dough
  5. Those small doughs is then kneaded in a circular sheets and is continuously beaten with an oily surface and rolled back to small balls
  6. then, those doughs are again kneaded and is cooked in the pan
  7. Finally, a soft and hot Parotta is ready to serve with a hot and spicy Kurma
